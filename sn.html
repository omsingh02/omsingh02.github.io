Controller Explanation (35 seconds)

"Thank you Harsh. The ESP32-WROOM-32 is the brain of our street light system. Think of it like your brain telling your body what to do - the ESP32 tells all other parts when to turn on, how bright to be, and what to do.

It's a tiny computer with 240 MHz dual-core processing, 520KB SRAM memory, WiFi connectivity, and 34 GPIO pins - enough to connect all our sensors. It operates on 3.3V and costs just ₹800."

Programming Logic (32 seconds)

"Our programming includes motion detection algorithms, weather analysis, emergency vehicle detection using sound sensor pattern recognition, and automatic brightness adjustment.

The controller processes sensor data every 10 seconds, makes environmental decisions in 0.5 seconds, and can activate emergency mode in under 1 second. It also handles cloud communication every 30 seconds for remote monitoring."

Motion Detection (35 seconds)

"Thanks Suhani. Our PIR motion sensors are like invisible eyes that detect movement. We use two PIR sensors for redundancy and better coverage. Each sensor detects motion in a 3-7 meter range with a 140-degree detection angle.

When someone walks past the street light, these sensors immediately notice the movement and tell the ESP32 brain 'Hey, someone's here - turn on the light!' They work perfectly even in complete darkness."

Technical Specs (32 seconds)

SPEAKER 6: Shaunaki Singh (Safety Protocols)

Time: 67 seconds | Slides: LDR Sensors + Ultrasonic Sensor

Light Detection (30 seconds)

"Thank you Piyush. LDR light sensors are our 'light detectors' that tell if it's day or night. During bright daylight, they say 'It's sunny, we don't need lights!' At night when dark, they say 'It's dark now, time to turn on lights!'

With resistance range 200-500kΩ and 540nm peak spectral response, they perfectly detect natural light conditions."

Distance Measurement (37 seconds)

"The HC-SR04 ultrasonic sensor works like 'bat vision' - sending invisible sound waves and listening for echoes. It detects objects from 2cm to 400cm with 3mm accuracy.

This sensor helps detect approaching vehicles and people at longer distances, enabling the system to prepare lighting before they arrive. Combined with our sound sensor for emergency vehicle detection, we can identify ambulances and fire trucks by their siren patterns and automatically activate full brightness in a 100-meter radius."

Weather Monitoring (35 seconds)

"Thanks Shaunaki. The DHT22 weather sensor is our 'weather reporter' that measures temperature and humidity. It operates from -40°C to 80°C with ±0.5°C accuracy and measures humidity from 0-100% RH with ±2% accuracy.

When it's foggy outside - humidity above 90% and temperature below 20°C - it tells the system to make lights warmer at 2700K color temperature so people can see better through fog."

Emergency Detection (32 seconds)

"Our INMP441 MEMS microphone is specifically configured for emergency vehicle detection. It uses digital I2S 24-bit processing with 61 dBA signal-to-noise ratio and 60 Hz to 15 kHz frequency response.

The microphone analyzes sound patterns to identify ambulance and fire truck sirens. When detected, the system instantly activates full brightness across a 100-meter radius, creating a lighting corridor for emergency vehicles. This ensures emergency responders have maximum visibility during critical situations."
